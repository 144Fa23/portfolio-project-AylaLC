My Classroom Management Philosophy

The keys to maintaining a well-managed classroom is building realtionships with students and increasing student engagement. As an educator, I will build relationships with my students by getting to know them. I will listen to stories about their lives, ask them questions about their interests, and actively observing their actions in the classroom. After I get to know my students, I will try to find ways to incorporate their interests into my lessons. That way, my students will be actively engaged in each lesson.

Non-negotiables

  • Respect everyone in the classroom! This includes the teacher, your peers, and yourself.
  • Come to class prepared with the proper materials. (A charged Chromebook, a pen/pencil, a notebook, and your textbook)
  • Before class begins, place your silenced cellphone into your backpack or face down on your desk.
  • Respect classroom materials by handling them gently, and replacing them to their correct position after use.
  • Work hard and remember to try your best!

Classroom procedures and expecations

Procedures for entering the classroom

  • Arrive on time before the bell rings
  • Come into class with a postive attitude
  • Walk to your seat calmly and quietly

Procedures for preparing for class to begin

  • Retrieve any materials needed out of your backpack
  • Place your silenced cellphone into your backpack or face down on your desk
  • Observe the lesson objectives on the board
  • Complete your bell ringer

Procedures during the lesson

  • Raise your hand if you have a question!
  • Respect the teacher by listening quietly as she speaks
  • Use class time wisely

Procedures for classroom dismissal

  • Begin putting your materials away when the teacher announces that the end of class is approaching
  • Return any classroom materials that are out of place
  • Clean up your area
  • Remain in your seat until the bell rings

Logical Consequences

Unfortunately, students do not always follow the rules. When this occurs, I believe that it is important for them to receive logical consequences. That way, they come to understand how to appropriately behave and why their actions were wrong. As an educator, I will never discipline a student with the intention of revenge. Instead, my goal will be to help my students learn appropriate behaviors.
